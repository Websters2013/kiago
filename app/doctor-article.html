Quality standards for Doctors

Quality standards for doctors

Our doctors make up the very core of our communities. You bring a unique medical perspective, expertise and passion, to the field of preventative healthcare and wellness. One main prerequisite in having you on board is the willingness to collaborate with your Kaigo team in order to educate and bring healing to your community. By doing so, you are able to offer members and other health partners an experience you wouldn’t be able to offer independently. To help ensure all doctors meet the community’s expectations, you must meet the standards listed below before members or partners can book Kaigo experiences and appointments with you.

These standards apply to your individual consultations as well as group experiences.

They will also help you earn great member reviews and partner recommendations. You can see how you’re doing by checking feedback from members and partners. Keep in mind that if you consistently receive low ratings or complaints or there are other indications that you do not meet these standards, your practice may be temporarily deactivated or removed from the platform. Please remember that we are here to help so always feel free to reach out to us if you are uncertain about any of these standards.

Standards

Doctors share their knowledge with members and health partners in their community, creating opportunities for all to gain new insights and develop a different viewpoint about healthy living. They’re excited to go out of their way to create a different healthcare experience.

  • Credibility.

    Doctors must maintain a license in the states they intend to serve and be able to demonstrate a deep knowledge of their stated expertise.

  • Access.

    Doctors should be willing to accommodate a member’s request for a virtual or in person appointment within 24 hours from the time requested.

  • Professional & Clean.

    Offices or clinics should always be kept very clean and organized and staff should friendly and be held to the highest standards of professionalism. We will stop by to inspect your space from time to time.

  • Perspective.

    Group experiences tell the doctor’s story so members become fully immersed in their world. Throughout the experience, doctors and/or their cohosting partners create moments that help members gain a deeper understanding of their expertise and viewpoint and make it fun.

  • Participation.

    Doctors provide opportunities for members to meaningfully engage in activities or conversations by fully taking part in the experiences, not just observing it.

  • Connection.

    Doctors are thoughtful about creating environments that feel inclusive. They get to know the individual (or group), encourage members to leave with new friends, and help everyone feel a sense of personal connection and community.

  • Accuracy & Claims.

    Based on the doctor’s description of their services in their profile, members should be clear on what to expect and outcomes. This includes who’s providing the service, the location of services provided, duration (how long the experience or service might take) is, and what guests will do. Only members who book through Kaigo can attend a Kaigo experience.

  • Communication.

    Doctors should be attentive and considerate when communicating with members, quickly responding when a member reaches out. Open, clear, and consistent communication lets a member know what to expect and helps them feel comfortable.

  • Commitment to appointment or experience.

    Once an appointment is confirmed, members trust the doctor to take care of their needs. Canceling or altering an appointment or experience can be disruptive to members. Doctors are expected to keep the description and availability of their schedule updated. Don’t double book.

  • Respect.

    Great doctors make each member feel included and welcomed in the community. They understand their members may come from different places, speak different languages and have different cultural perspectives. They treat all members with dignity or respect.

  • Value.

    You have control over how your price your service, but it’s important to set a price that balances expectations with what your service or experience has to offer. After attending an appointment or group experience, members should feel satisfied with the amount of time and money they’ve spent. Based on your pricing, members should be able to anticipate the level of service and exclusivity they’ll encounter.

  • Personalization.

    Doctors at Kaigo think about every member as unique individuals and treat them specifically based on their needs. Members expect treatment plans that are tailored to fit their specific needs and not generalized recommendations.
